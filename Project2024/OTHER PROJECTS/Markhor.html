Kashmir Markhor Recovery Project



The Kashmir Markhor Recovery Project is a dedicated conservation initiative aimed at reviving the population of the Kashmir markhor (Capra falconeri cashmiriensis), a wild goat species native to the mountainous regions of northern India and Pakistan. The project addresses the severe decline in markhor numbers due to poaching, habitat destruction, and competition with livestock.
Launched in collaboration between government agencies, non-governmental organizations, and local communities, the project's primary goals include habitat restoration, anti-poaching measures, and community engagement. By enforcing stricter wildlife protection laws and employing local rangers, the project aims to curb illegal hunting and safeguard markhor habitats.
Habitat restoration efforts focus on reforestation and controlling overgrazing to ensure sufficient food resources for the markhor. Additionally, the project works with local communities to reduce human-wildlife conflict and promote sustainable livelihoods, such as eco-tourism, that benefit both the people and the wildlife.
The project has seen positive outcomes, with gradual increases in markhor populations and improved habitat conditions.
