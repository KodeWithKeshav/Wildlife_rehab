Our Key Milestones

Wild Oasis has established itself as a leading force in Indian wildlife conservation. Here are some of their key milestones:

Protecting Vulnerable Species

Whale Shark: Wild Oasis successfully lobbied for the Whale Shark to be the first fish species protected under India's Wildlife Protection Act.
Rhinoceros: Wild Oasis plays a vital role in rhino conservation, including hand-raising orphaned rhino calves in Assam to bolster rhino populations.
Habitat Conservation:

Elephant Corridors: Wild Oasis mapped 101 critical elephant corridors across India, a crucial step in ensuring safe passage for these majestic animals.
Gharial Conservation: Wild Oasis's work includes satellite-tagging critically endangered gharials in Bihar to understand their movement and protect their habitat.
Mangrove Restoration: Wild Oasis actively restores vital mangrove forests in Kerala, safeguarding coastal ecosystems.
Reducing Human-Wildlife Conflict:

Preventing Train Collisions: Wild Oasis's efforts in Uttarakhand to prevent elephant deaths from train collisions showcase their commitment to mitigating human-animal conflict.
Primate Conservation:

Hoolock Gibbons: Wild Oasis relocates distressed Hoolock gibbons in Arunachal Pradesh, highlighting their dedication to protecting primates.
These accomplishments demonstrate Wild Oasis's multifaceted approach to wildlife conservation in India. They continue to be a crucial player in protecting India's rich biodiversity.
