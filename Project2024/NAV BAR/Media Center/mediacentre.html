Conservation Science

As the world's leading conservation organization, we work in nearly 100 countries to tackle the most critical issues at the intersection of nature, people, and climate.

Our goals:

Recognizing that the problems facing our planet are increasingly more complex and urgent, WWF focuses its work on six ambitious goals. Through this integrative approach, we can challenge the planet's greatest threats and ensure a healthy future for people and nature.

Field Work

Our field work involves working directly in the communities and habitats where conservation efforts are needed the most. We collaborate with local stakeholders, conduct research, and implement on-the-ground projects to protect and restore ecosystems, conserve wildlife, and promote sustainable livelihoods.

Through our field work, we aim to address the specific challenges faced by different regions and species, and contribute to the overall conservation goals of our organization. We engage with local communities, raise awareness about environmental issues, and empower people to become stewards of their natural resources.

Policy Advocacy

Policy advocacy is a crucial aspect of our work. We strive to influence policies and decision-making processes at local, national, and international levels to promote sustainable development and conservation.

Through research, analysis, and engagement with policymakers, we aim to shape policies that prioritize environmental protection, biodiversity conservation, and the well-being of communities. We advocate for stronger regulations, sustainable practices, and the integration of environmental considerations into various sectors.

Our policy advocacy efforts also involve collaborating with other organizations, forming alliances, and participating in global platforms to amplify our impact and drive positive change. By advocating for policies that support nature conservation and address pressing environmental challenges, we contribute to building a more sustainable and resilient future for all.
