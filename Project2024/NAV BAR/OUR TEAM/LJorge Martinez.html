LJorge is a southwest Virginia native, growing up with a fascination of the beautiful Appalachian Mountains and of the wildlife that call those old hills home.
he graduated with a degree in Biology from James Madison University in 2008, taking an internship at a local wildlife hospital.
Lacy ended up working at that same hospital, the Wildlife Center of Virginia, for 13 years and in different positions, finally settling into the role of Vice President for Administration and overseeing budgeting, finances, and operations. he now lives in Raleigh, North Carolina, and is the Director of Finance and Operations for the North Carolina Wildlife Federation, where Lacy appreciates contributing to the work being done to conserve and protect wildlife habitat.
Lacy is pursuing a master's degree in Business Administration, enjoys reading, hiking, and spying on birds and other wildlife. he is excited to support OASIS in its mission to improve and promote wildlife rehabilitation by serving on the Board of Directors.
